## Strategic Context

**From Personalized Targeting to Personalized Language**

AT&T's ML-powered psychographic personalization program made it possible to decide *who* should see *what* across experiences serving approximately 30 million monthly users. But deciding what to show is only half of personalization — the message itself still had to be written. Producing distinct, on-brand copy variations for each psychographic audience remained a manual effort, which capped how much of the personalization capability could actually be used.

Atlas is a generative AI capability I led to close that gap: producing psychographic copy variations at scale, so the volume of message variants could keep pace with the sophistication of the targeting.

## The Challenge

**Copy Was the Bottleneck**

  - Each psychographic segment needed its own message framing to realize the value of the targeting model
  - Manual copywriting could not produce variations at the volume and speed the personalization program demanded
  - Every unwritten variation was unrealized value: audiences received generic copy even when the system knew better

## The Solution

**Generative Copy, Grounded in Psychographics**

Atlas applies generative AI to produce copy variations aligned to psychographic audience profiles, giving marketing and experience teams a scalable way to match message to audience. Variations feed the same experimentation discipline used across the personalization program, so generated copy earns its place through measured performance rather than assumption.

## My Role

I led Atlas as the product lead: shaping the capability around measurable business outcomes, building the case for investment, and translating between business stakeholders and the engineering and data science partners who built and productionized the models. Consistent with how I ship AI products, Atlas was validated through experimentation before scaling.

## Results & Impact

**Measured Outcomes**

Headline outcomes across the applicable prepaid and accessories experiences:

  - 17% productivity improvement in producing audience-specific copy variations
  - 25% engagement improvement
  - 15% revenue improvement

Beyond the metrics, Atlas turned copy production from a constraint on the personalization program into a capability that scales with it.
